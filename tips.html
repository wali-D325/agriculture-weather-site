Farming Tips

Evidence-based advice for smallholder farmers in Malawi

💧 Water Management
🌅
Water
Water Early or Late
Water crops at 6–8am or after 5pm to reduce evaporation by up to 30% and prevent leaf scorch from midday heat.
💧
Water
Drip Irrigation Saves Water
Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the roots, reducing usage by up to 50% compared to flood irrigation while improving yields.
🌱 Soil Health
🔄
Soil
Rotate Crops Every Season
Rotate maize → beans → groundnuts to restore soil nitrogen, break pest cycles, and maintain long-term productivity.
🪱
Soil
Add Organic Matter
Compost, crop residues, and animal manure enrich soil structure and feed the beneficial microorganisms that boost crop yields.
🧪
Soil
Test Your Soil First
A soil test reveals pH and nutrient levels before you spend money on fertilizer. Over-application wastes money and acidifies the soil.
🌾 Planting
🌱
Planting
Choose the Right Variety
Use certified, drought-tolerant or disease-resistant seeds suited to your region. Local extension officers can recommend the best options for your district.
📐
Planting
Correct Plant Spacing
Proper spacing (90cm × 30cm for maize) maximises light, air, and nutrient access per plant. Use the Tools tab in AgriWeather Pro+ to calculate exact numbers.
⏰
Planting
Time Planting with Rain
Plant 1–3 days before expected rainfall to ensure excellent germination and reduce irrigation needs by up to 40%.
🐛 Pest & Disease
🧹
Pest Control
Keep Fields Clean
Remove weeds, dead plants, and crop debris regularly — these are breeding grounds for pests and diseases that spread quickly during warm, humid weather.
🐞
Pest Control
Try Natural Pest Control First
Use neem oil spray, companion planting, and beneficial insects before resorting to chemicals. This protects soil health and significantly reduces input costs.
📦 Post-Harvest & Market
📦
Post-Harvest
Dry Grain Properly
Dry harvested grain to below 13% moisture before storage. Use hermetic (airtight) bags or metal silos to prevent aflatoxin and weevil damage that can destroy an entire harvest.
📈
Market
Time Your Crop Sales
Crop prices are always lowest right after the harvest season. If you can store grain safely for 2–3 months, you can typically sell at significantly higher prices. Check the Market tab in AgriWeather Pro+ to monitor price trends.
